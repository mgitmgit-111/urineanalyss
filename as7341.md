AS7341 reality check first
The AS7341 covers 415–680nm (8 optical channels + clear + NIR). For urine strip analysis this means:
Pads you can read reliably: GLU, PRO, KET, URO, BIL — these react in the visible range where AS7341 is strong.
Pads that are marginal: NIT, LEU — their color reactions peak in ranges where AS7341 has weaker sensitivity. You'll need very tight optical control to get clinical agreement here.
Pads that are problematic: SG (specific gravity) — measured refractometrically or by color shift at ~700nm+. The AS7341's NIR channel (910nm) is a single broadband bucket, not a proper NIR channel. SG will be your hardest pad.
Recommendation: Don't try to do SG with the AS7341. Either drop it from the device's intended use (report 9 pads, not 10) or add a separate refractometer module. Narrowing intended use is standard regulatory strategy and makes your clinical validation easier.

Priority focus areas — ranked by impact on accuracy
1. Optical enclosure — 60% of your accuracy lives here
The AS7341 has a built-in LED but do not use it for production. It's too close to the sensor, creates specular reflection off the pad surface, and has no intensity control. Instead:

Mount a white LED ring (5000K, CRI >95) at 45° angle of incidence, 8–10mm from pad surface. 45°/0° geometry is the IVD industry standard for reflectance reading.
The sensor looks straight down at 0°. This eliminates specular glare.
Enclosure must be machined or injection-moulded black POM (polyoxymethylene) — matte black, zero reflectance. 3D printed is fine for prototyping but FDM surfaces scatter light internally.
The strip cradle indexes off the strip's bottom edge and one side edge — two hard stops, spring-loaded pressure from the other two sides. This gives you <0.3mm repeatability.
Total internal volume should be <15cm³. Smaller = less stray light path.

2. Calibration protocol — 30% of your accuracy
Every single reading session needs this sequence, implemented in sensor_reader.py on the Pi:
Power on LED → wait 2000ms for thermal stabilisation
Read dark current (LED off) → store as D[ch] for 8 channels
Read white PTFE tile → store as W[ch]
Read strip lot barcode → load lot reference values from calibration.json
For each pad read:
    Raw = sensor reading R[ch]
    Normalized = (R[ch] - D[ch]) / (W[ch] - D[ch])
    Temp-corrected = Normalized × temp_correction_factor(T)
